About

Independent counsel.
Undivided attention.

Lakshmi Sreedhar is an advocate based in Kerala. She graduated from the National University of Advanced Legal Studies (NUALS) in 2016 and has been practising since — appearing before the High Court of Kerala, the Supreme Court of India, and courts and forums across the country.

Her practice covers a wide range — constitutional and administrative law, civil and criminal litigation, corporate and commercial matters, matrimonial matters, intellectual property, media law, banking, consumer, arbitration, and more. She also takes up pro bono matters.

Questions

Common questions.

Do I need to prepare anything before contacting you?

No. A WhatsApp message with a brief description of your situation is enough — no documents or formalities required to start.

What areas do you practise in?

Primarily the High Court of Kerala and the Supreme Court of India, with appearances before courts and forums across the country — covering constitutional, civil and criminal, corporate, matrimonial, property, IP, media, banking, consumer, arbitration, and panchayat-law matters.

How soon can I get an appointment?

Initial response via WhatsApp is usually within a few hours. Appointments are scheduled based on availability, typically within a few days.

Are consultations confidential?

Yes. All discussions are treated with strict professional confidentiality from the very first message.

Do you offer online consultations?

Yes — video call appointments are available in addition to in-person meetings at the Kochi office.

Do you take on pro bono matters?

Yes. Pro bono work is a meaningful part of the practice, particularly involving child rights, gender-based violence, and access-to-justice matters.
